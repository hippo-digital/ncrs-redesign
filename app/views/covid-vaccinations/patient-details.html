Reasonable Adjustments

This patient does not show a requirement for Reasonable Adjustments

The NHS has a legal duty to make it easy for disabled and non-disabled individuals to use health and care services, as stated by the Equality Act (2010) (opens in a new tab).

A Reasonable Adjustments record ensures patients with physical and mental impairments have equal access to healthcare services.

Read more about Reasonable Adjustments

A Reasonable Adjustments record can show potential adjustments for a patient. These may include (but aren't limited to):

  • communication needs (supported by the Accessibility Standard (opens in a new tab))
  • adjustments to the environment
  • impairments, to help undersyand the necessary adjustments

If you believe this patient requires a Reasonable Adjustments record, you can create one.
